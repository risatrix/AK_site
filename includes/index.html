Aristophanes'

Women in Congress

 

A provocative new translation by Drs. Amanda Krauss and Jess Miner

 

Two millennia ago, Aristophanes wondered what would happen if women took over the government. A communist utopia, of course, with sexual satisfaction guaranteed for every citizen!

We wonder if the idea of women in power is as unimaginable now as it was in 392 BCE, when Aristophanes' play was first performed, and we hope our translation will inspire modern readers to think about the question -- after they stop laughing, that is!

As The New York Times recently observed, "comedy...may be the best outlet for stirring debate." Having tested our translation in college classrooms and in performance, we know this to be true: we have witnessed the play's ability both to entertain and to provoke serious discussion. We are now eager to introduce Aristophanes, the ancient equivalent of Jon Stewart, to the world at large.

Amanda Krauss was an Assistant Professor at Vanderbilt University. Her interests include the history of comedy and humor theory.

Jess Miner was an Assistant Professor at the College of Charleston. She specializes in Greek Oratory, Middle Comedy, and Ancient and Modern Rhetoric.

Drs. Krauss and Miner both believe strongly in the need for accessible translations of ancient plays, the better to demonstrate the relevance of Classics to a modern audience.
